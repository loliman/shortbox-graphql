Text with art/photosQuestions that need answers! Which Marvel creator/employee do you think is a Skrull?
Marc Sumerak - Writer (Franklin Richards): Tom Brevoort. He knows FAR too much about every Marvel hero and villain! It's a sure sign he's not only part of the Skrull conspiracy... he's actually planning it! I bet if we shaved off his famous beard we'd find a very familiar alien chin underneath...
Fred van Lente - Writer (Super-Villain Team-Up, Incredible Hercules): Nathan Cosby, without a doubt. No one could possibly know that much about college football and not be a Skrull.
Brian Reed - Writer (Captain Marvel (VII) (Khn'nr), Ms. Marvel (II), New Avengers: Illuminati (II)): I promised I wouldn't tell, but Jen Grünwald is a Skrull. But it's okay, because she's not really into the "destroy the Earth" thing as much as she is into having fun. So, yeah, she's a Skrull, be we're totally safe from her.
Matt Fraction - Writer (The Order (I), The Punisher War Journal (II), The Immortal Iron Fist): Nick Lowe. Only a shapeshifter could rock a beard like that.
Mike Carey - Writer (X-Men (II), Ultimate Fantastic Four): Well I always assumed that Brubaker was because he's got the chin and the ears and the high-tech alien gadgets. But wait, you're trying to tell me there's only one? Yeah, that's what you'd LIKE us to think!
Brian Michael Bendis - Writer (The New Avengers (I), Mighty Avengers, Secret Invasion, Ultimate Spider-Man (I), Ultimate Origins, Powers (I)): I have hard evidence that all of you are. All of which I will reveal in Secret Invasion #7, if I live to tell the - aacckk! Jen Grünwald choking me with her ever changing hair. A clue so obvious... I should.. have.. ak!
Ed Brubaker - Writer (Captain America (V), Daredevil (II), The Immortal Iron Fist, Criminal (I), Uncanny X-Men (I)): Matt Fraction - Because only a Skrull would be able to cross over into mainstream comis as quickly as Matt has, and still retain his "indie" cred.
Bryan Hitch - Artist (Ultimates 3, Fantastic Four (I) cont.): Can't be any of 'em. Let's face it, if you were a shape-shifter you would never choose to look like any Marvel creator or employee, would you? Why on earth would anybody choose to look like Bendis or Millar? You just wouldn't. Nobody, even an alien, would ever choose Warren Ellis as a human template... John Romita Jr. maybe; he WAS Hunk of the Month once...
Peter David - Writer (X-Factor (III), She-Hulk (II)): Ralph Macchio. No one can last that long at Marvel without being a master of disguise.
Chris Eliopoulos - Artist, Letterer (Franklin Richards): Ralph Macchio. I mean, who could work that long for Marvel and still look that young. Plus, he looks nothing like the Karate Kid.
J. Michael Straczynski - Writer (Thor (III), The Twelve, The Amazing Spider-Man (I) cont.): I think the correct question would be, which of them ISN'T a Skrull? I have my reasons.
Mark Brooks - Artist (Ultimate Fantastic Four): I gotta go with Ultimate group editor Ralph Macchio. He's so mild-mannered and stays under the radar so much that he's got to be a Skrull sleeper agent. How else could you explain his fascination with Gwyneth Paltrow?
Jeff Parker - Writer (X-Men: First Class (II)): I don't think, I KNOW Mark Paniccia is a Skrull. He looks much younger than he should, and one day I saw him relax and his chin went all rippled. Also at the end of the day he often mentions needing to get back to his homeworld, and then always catches himself and says 'I mean, home.'
Joe Quesada - Marvel Editor-in-Chief, Artist (The Amazing Spider-Man (I) cont.): Since Jim McCann has circulated this questionnaire, I believe he's diverting attention from himself and the fact that he is the one true Master Skrull.
Robert Kirkmann - Writer (Marvel Zombies 3, Ultimate X-Men): Dan Slott, it's gotta be him. I'm not saying his work on She-Hulk (II) was bad, in fact I'd go as far as to say it was absolutely amazing, but the work he's doing now, with Avengers: The Initiative and the upcoming The Amazing Spider-Man (I) cont. run, is such a leap in terms of his ability -- he's gotta be a Skrull. No doubt about it.
Frank Cho - Artist, Writer (Mighty Avengers): Joe Quesada. His haircut is a dead giveaway.
